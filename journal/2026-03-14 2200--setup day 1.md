# Journal — 2026-03-14

## First entry. Why this exists.

This repo started from a conversation about $80 in API costs over a few days, and a question: *can the agent self-organize, or is that an unrealistic expectation?*

The honest answer was: both. Some of the cost was a config bug (short cache TTL, now fixed). Some was legitimate — complex work costs real money. And some was structural: no scoping, no model routing, no sub-agent pattern. The agent doing everything at full cost with no guardrails.

The deeper question was whether the expectation of self-organization was realistic. It is — but it requires explicit policy, not just trust. An LLM doesn't manage its own costs unprompted. A well-configured system with clear rules can.

## What we're building

Three things, in order of impact:

1. **Model routing** — Haiku by default, Sonnet when it earns it. Written into AGENTS.md as policy the agent follows autonomously. Decision log maintained in `model-routing-policy.md`.

2. **Sub-agent execution** — Scoped tasks go to sub-agents, not the main session. Main session stays lean. Failure doesn't pollute the conversation.

3. **Periodic self-review** — Agent reviews its own session logs, finds patterns, proposes AGENTS.md changes. Human approves before anything is applied. No silent self-modification.

## Why publish it

Most published OpenClaw setups are by developers. This one is by a finance professional using AI as operational infrastructure. Different problems, different solutions, and probably more relatable to the large slice of professionals who aren't coders.

The goal isn't to showcase a finished setup. It's to document an honest journey — including the mistakes.

## Philosophy

*Efficiency and effectiveness on a foundation of achievable expectations.*

The foundation comes first. E&E are what you build on top of it.
